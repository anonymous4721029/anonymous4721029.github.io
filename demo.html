Polyphonic rhythm transcription

Abstract

A rhythm trancription algorithm converts a MIDI performance to a music score representation. We show results of comparing three HMM-based algorithms; note HMM [1,2], metrical HMM [3,4], and merged-output HMM [5]. As an evaluation measure, we use the rhythm correction cost (RCC), which is defined as the least number of editions to correct a estimated score. For details, see our paper [5].

Demonstrations

Examples of rhythm transcription are shown. Each algorithm outputs quantised score times of the notes in an input MIDI performance. Score typesetting (e.g. beaming) is done manually to make the results clearer. For the merged-output HMM, the notes are grouped into two voices, which are shown in the upper and lower staves.

Good examples

Examples in which the merged-output HMM outperformed other HMMs.
